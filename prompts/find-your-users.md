# Find Your Users — AI Community & Thread Research Prompt

Find the exact places online where your target users are already talking about the problem you solve — Reddit threads, forums, Facebook groups, and X/Twitter posts — and get a ranked, ready-to-act playbook for joining the conversation genuinely.

> **Best results:** run this in the deepest research mode available — ChatGPT or Gemini **Deep Research**, or Claude's **extended thinking**. Swap the four `[BRACKETS]` for your product before you run it.

---

## The prompt

```text
Find Reddit communities and threads, online forums, Facebook groups, and X/Twitter posts where my target users discuss the problem my product solves.

My product, [PRODUCT], helps [WHO IT'S FOR] by [WHAT IT DOES]. Specifically, it [KEY FEATURES].

Look for people expressing the pain my product solves — asking things like "is there an app for this," "what's the best way to do X," or "why is it so hard to do Y." Capture the actual language frustrated users use when they're hunting for a solution like mine.

For each result, give me:
- the platform
- the link
- why it's relevant
- the pain point the person is expressing
- how my product could naturally fit into the conversation
- whether I should comment, DM, post, or just observe
- the subreddit / group size and how recent and active the thread is

Prioritize communities where I can contribute genuinely without sounding promotional. Rank them best-first so I know where to start, and suggest 5–10 search terms I can use to find more.
```

---

## How to use it

1. **Fill in the four `[BRACKETS]`** with your product details:
   - `[PRODUCT]` — your product's name
   - `[WHO IT'S FOR]` — your target user
   - `[WHAT IT DOES]` — the core outcome you deliver
   - `[KEY FEATURES]` — the specifics that make it useful
2. **Turn on deep / extended research mode.**
3. **Run it**, then start with the top-ranked communities.
4. **Contribute genuinely** — answer questions, be helpful, build credibility — *before* you ever mention your product.
